Tree Therapist
Brainstorm Series
Luke walks into the woods and sits with a different tree each episode. The tree is the therapist. Luke is the patient. The tree vents — about deforestation, about humans, about the state of the planet — and Luke just listens and occasionally responds. Dry, absurdist, scripted. Each episode is a new species, a new personality.
Opening hook (working) "I've been standing here for 80 years. Let's talk about what I've seen."
Format: Scripted dialogue, Luke on camera with a tree
Length: 30–60s per episode
Crew: Luke on camera, Landon films
Each tree species gets its own personality. The oak is stoic. The willow is dramatic. The birch is anxious. Could run indefinitely.
Climbing Trees & Rating Them
Brainstorm Series
Luke and Landon climb a different tree each episode and rate the species on a scale of 1–10 — climbability, branch structure, vibe, bark texture, whatever criteria they invent. Completely deadpan review format. The brand is present but incidental.
Opening hook (working) "We rate trees. Today: the white oak."
Format: Review / rating format, both founders on camera
Length: 20–40s per episode
Crew: Luke + Landon, phone propped or tripod
The rating criteria are part of the joke — they can be ridiculous. "Spiritual energy: 6/10." Gives Landon strong screen presence alongside Luke.

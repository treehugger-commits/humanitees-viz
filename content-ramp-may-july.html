Where the Intern Fits
Recommended start: June, week 5 or 6. May is about building the pipeline and learning what works. Bringing someone in before the pattern is clear means training them on the wrong things. Get one month of data first.
What they own from day one
  • Second camera — films Luke and Landon from an outside angle while they do their own handheld POV
  • B-roll capture — product detail, lifestyle context, environmental texture. The stuff founders don't have time to think about mid-shoot
  • Raw footage org — ingesting, labeling, dropping into Drive structure so Frank and Jewel aren't chasing files
  • First-pass selects — marking the 3–5 best takes from each session so editors start from a shortlist
What they grow into (July+)
  • Owning a filming session solo — brief from Luke, go shoot, return with organized footage
  • First-pass rough cuts for one account (App or Brand, not both at first)
  • Trend monitoring — watching the zeitgeist weekly and flagging relevant formats to Luke before Monday ideation
  • Ambassador content coordination — reaching out to ambassadors for UGC, organizing what comes back
What to look for in the hire
  • Shows you their own TikTok or IG account — they should have a point of view on what works, not just a degree
  • Can hold a phone steady and think about framing intuitively — not a trained filmmaker, but not oblivious either
  • Has edited short-form video before (CapCut, Premiere, doesn't matter — just evidence they've done it)
  • Genuinely interested in the brand, not just a line on a resume — the app content only works if the person filming it understands what they're selling
  • Available at least 15–20 hours/week consistently — a 5-hour-a-week intern breaks the pipeline
Why June, not May
May is the test month. You'll learn which pillars resonate, which hooks tank, and how the editing loop between Frank/Jewel and the founders actually runs in practice. Hiring in May means onboarding someone before you know what the job is. Start the hire process in May — post the role, run interviews — so they're in seat by June 1 or 2.
